## Part 2: List-Building — Ethical Opt-In Only

WhatsApp is a personal, permission-based channel. **NEVER add a number to your broadcast list without explicit consent.**

### How to Build Your Broadcast List (Ethical Methods Only)

1. **Order-confirmation opt-in checkbox (website/Razorpay flow):**
   - Add an opt-in checkbox at checkout: "Send me festive drops and exclusive offers on WhatsApp."
   - Pre-ticked checkboxes are fine (as long as the user can uncheck).
   - Store consented numbers in a "WhatsApp Opt-In" list.

2. **"Message us to join festive drops" story CTA:**
   - Post an Instagram Story: "Want festival offers + early drops sent to your WhatsApp? Message us 'JOIN' to opt in 💛"
   - When they message, manually add them to your broadcast list.
   - Confirm their opt-in: "You're in! We'll send you festive offers on WhatsApp. Reply STOP anytime to opt out."

3. **NEVER scrape or buy numbers:**
   - Do NOT add customer phone numbers from orders, shipping labels, or invoices without explicit opt-in.
   - Do NOT buy phone lists, scrape Instagram followers' numbers, or guess numbers from handles.
   - WhatsApp's policy is strict: unsolicited messages = account suspension risk.

4. **Broadcast list vs. group distinction:**
   - **Broadcast list:** recipients see the message as if you sent it one-on-one. They don't see who else received it. Perfect for festival offers.
   - **WhatsApp group:** everyone sees everyone, chaotic, not suitable for broadcast campaigns.
   - Use **broadcast lists** for all festival sends.

5. **Broadcast list 256-contact limit:**
   - WhatsApp Business allows up to 256 contacts per broadcast list.
   - If your list exceeds 256, create multiple broadcast lists (e.g., "Broadcast List 1 — Rakhi", "Broadcast List 2 — Rakhi").
   - Or segment by region, order history, or product interest.

6. **WhatsApp policy caution — keep personal tone:**
   - Broadcast lists only reach contacts who have **saved your business number** in their phone.
   - If they haven't saved your number, they won't receive the broadcast.
   - **Solution:** In your first message to a new opt-in, say: "Hi [Name]! Save this number as 'Tathastu Keepsakes' so you never miss a festive drop 💛"
   - Avoid copy-paste spam vibes — personalize `[Name]`, keep tone warm, invite replies.
   - Too many blocks/spam reports = WhatsApp may suspend your account. Keep it personal, opt-in-only, and respectful.

7. **No automation/API:**
   - All WhatsApp sends are **manual** — paste the message into WhatsApp Business and send to broadcast lists.
   - Do NOT use third-party automation tools, bots, or unofficial WhatsApp API wrappers (they violate WhatsApp's Terms of Service and risk account bans).
   - Official WhatsApp Business API (the paid enterprise solution) is overkill for this scale — stick to WhatsApp Business app (free) + manual sends.

## Part 3: WhatsApp Status Cadence + 6 Reusable Status Ideas

### Status Cadence During Festival Bursts

**Mirror your Instagram Stories cadence** during festival bursts:

- **Festival week (T−7 to festival day):** Post 2–3 WhatsApp Status updates per day.
  - Morning (9–10 AM): Product spotlight or countdown.
  - Evening (5–7 PM): Behind-the-scenes, customer love, or ship cut-off reminder.

- **Off-season (non-festival weeks):** Post 1–2 Status updates per week.
  - Keep the brand visible without overwhelming your audience.

**Status lifespan:** 24 hours (same as Instagram Stories). Status is seen by contacts who have saved your number.

---
